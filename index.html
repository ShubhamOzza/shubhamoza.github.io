About

Background

I got into quantitative finance from the operations side — joining Landytech out of grad school and quickly ending up responsible for the data accuracy of portfolios worth over a billion dollars. That forced me to get rigorous fast about how financial data actually works: where it breaks, why it breaks, and how to build systems that catch it before it reaches a client.

At Capgemini, I moved into OTC derivatives — collateral management, ISDA agreements, margin exposure monitoring for a US investment bank. That's where I started building real intuition for how risk positions are constructed and measured.

I'm now at UT Dallas studying Financial Technology & Analytics, filling in the mathematical foundations — probability theory, stochastic modeling, ML for finance — and applying them to projects in alpha generation and options pricing. The goal is to move from someone who monitors quantitative outputs to someone who builds them.
