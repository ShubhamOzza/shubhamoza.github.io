About

Background

I started my career in financial operations and slowly worked my way toward the quantitative side of things. Four years across IBM, Capgemini, and Landytech gave me a ground-level view of how institutional finance actually runs — the messy parts, the data problems, the gap between what models assume and what systems deliver.

Most of that time was spent on the infrastructure behind investment decisions: reconciling portfolios, validating OTC derivative exposures, building ETL pipelines, and getting performance numbers right for clients who manage serious capital. At Landytech I was responsible for analytics across $1.2B+ AUM spanning equities, fixed income, hedge funds, and private equity.

I came to UT Dallas because I wanted to understand the math that sits behind those decisions. The MS in Financial Technology and Analytics is letting me build that foundation properly — options pricing, time-series modeling, machine learning in finance, and the quantitative methods I was applying intuitively in practice.

Right now I'm focused on quant research and derivatives analytics. The projects on this site reflect where my thinking is going.
