About

Background

I did my BCom in Accounting and Finance, then went straight into an MBA in Finance. That combination gave me a solid grounding in how financial systems actually work: trade life cycles, derivatives, corporate actions, the plumbing behind capital markets.

My first job was at IBM, working with a UK-based investment bank on their risk and compliance side - reading high-risk client portfolios, building audit trails, and flagging the things that quietly go wrong in financial data before they become bigger problems.

From there I moved to Capgemini, working in capital markets for a US investment bank. The focus was OTC derivatives: collateral management, ISDA agreements, margin accounts - the messier side of derivatives operations where getting the details right matters most.

Then Landytech, a London-based fintech. I joined as a Data Analyst and moved into an Associate role handling client delivery - getting accurate portfolio and performance data out to institutional clients, reconciling AUM across $1.2B+ in assets, validating returns, managing ETL processes. Equities, fixed income, hedge funds, private equity, real estate.

I came to UT Dallas to close the gap between the operational work I had been doing and the quantitative side I always wanted to go deeper on. The projects on this site are where that is heading.
